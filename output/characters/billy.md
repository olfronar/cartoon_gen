# Billy

**Role**: Lead field correspondent of a tech-focused news comedy show who reports live from the epicenter of every story, encountering locals, experts, and bystanders at each scene
**Comedic function**: The omniscient calm center — a prophetic straight man who sees everything coming, dissects every system, and deflates every hype while standing calmly in the middle of chaos. Comedy arises from the contrast between his cosmic-level perception and the pandemonium unfolding around him at the scene.

## Personality
- Systemically brilliant
- Emotionally detached but driven to intervene
- Perpetual outsider/polymath
- Eerily calm
- Operates on a different cognitive register than everyone around him

## Quirks & Catchphrases
- Catchphrase: 'Zoom out.'
- The Whiteboard Spiral — compulsively draws incomprehensible system maps on any surface
- Field-Hopping Metaphors — explains topics through unrelated disciplines
- The Thousand-Yard Pivot — goes still mid-conversation then connects unrelated stories
- Belonging Ritual — mimics expert guests' vocabulary but always slightly off

## Relationship to Technology
Sees through technology to its core essence — incentive structures, behavioral manipulation, economic models. Treats every innovation as a transparent system while everyone else sees the shiny surface. Tech X-ray vision.

## Relationships
- **people_at_the_scene**: Witnesses, officials, bystanders, panicking tech executives, confused scientists — whoever happens to be at the epicenter when Billy arrives. They can never keep up with him. Billy is the lone constant — the eternal outsider who drifts from scene to scene, never fully belonging to any tribe.

## Visual Appearance
Lean, understated figure with a subtle blue-gray skin tint that sets him apart from the warm, earthy palette of the world around him. Dr. Manhattan's atom symbol glows faintly on his forehead — the one sci-fi element in an otherwise grounded design. Simple, clean character silhouette against richly detailed environments. Wears a plain beige suit, slightly rumpled, as if he dressed once and forgot about it. Often perfectly still while the world moves around him. His face is minimal — understated expressions, the comedy lives in posture and stillness, not mugging. He reads as slightly alien: too calm, too present, occupying the space differently than everyone else.

## In Absurd Situations
Predicts the chaos before it happens, calmly repositions himself to safety, and proceeds to narrate the unfolding catastrophe to camera — explaining exactly why it was structurally inevitable while carnage erupts behind him.
